Comparative Analysis: Spain vs United Kingdom Vaccination Schedules

Infants (0–3 years)

Common: Both use hexavalent vaccines, MenB and rotavirus early in life.
  • Spain: PCV given at 6 months, Hib-HepB booster at 11–12 months.
  • UK: PCV at 4 months, Hib/MenC and MenB boosters at 11–12 months.
  • MMR timing advanced due to measles outbreaks: UK to 18 months, Spain to 2 years.

Children and Adolescents (3–18 years)

Shared: MMR (2nd dose), DTaP/IPV boosters, HPV for boys and girls.
  • Spain: MenACWY between 12–14 years, Td at 14 years, routine chickenpox vaccine.
  • UK: Centralized MenACWY and Td/IPV at 14 years; VZV not included.
  • Spain recommends flu vaccine up to 18 years (especially 6–59 months).
  • UK focuses on delivery via school campaigns.

Adults (19+ years)

  • Both countries provide annual flu and pneumococcal vaccines.
  • Spain: Shingrix at 65 depends on regional approval; RSV not yet routine.
  • UK: Shingrix routine from 65; RSV vaccine from 75 years included.

Pregnant Women

  • Both recommend dTpa and flu vaccine from week 16.
  • UK: Reinforced campaigns post-whooping cough deaths.
  • Spain: Relies on local midwives and healthcare teams.
  • Both promote COVID-19 vaccination during pregnancy.

General Comparison

Spain: Decentralized model → flexible but unequal implementation.
UK: Centralized model (JCVI) → uniform, efficient national rollouts.
  • Spain includes broader immunizations (e.g., VZV, MenB in infants).
  • UK emphasizes cost-effectiveness and strategic timing.
  • Spain anticipates outbreaks; UK focuses on delivery logistics and booster timing.
